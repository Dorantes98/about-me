Assignment 3, Nov 14

Challenges

One of the biggest challenges in this assignment was implementing the filtering system so it worked with both the original posts and any new posts I created. Initially, the filtering logic worked well for the original posts, but I struggled with getting it to re-filter correctly once new posts were added. Every time I applied a filter, it would only consider the currently displayed posts, which led to issues when I wanted to change filters or clear them altogether. when I tried to remove all filters and update so all the posts came back, they wouldn't come back.

The solution involved creating a single array, allPosts, to keep track of all the posts including created ones. By iterating over allPosts each time I applied a filter, all the posts were considered every time, even if they had been removed from the DOM based on previous filtering.

What I Learned

I learned during this assignment how important it is to maintain a data structure, like my array, in order to keep track of all the posts on the page. By storing all posts in a single array, I could manage filtering without constantly modifying the DOM directly. I also gained a better understanding of JavaScript event handling and DOM manipulation, especially with dynamically added elements. Another insight was the importance of thinking through edge cases like clearing filters or re-applying filters multiple times with different criteria.
